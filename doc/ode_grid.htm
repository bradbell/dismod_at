Purpose
The ordinary differential equation is solved along cohort lines, i.e., lines where time minus age is constant. On the other hand, data points are specified for a rectangular interval in age and time; see age_lower and age_upper . In order to compute the model for the data A_i we solve the ODE on a grid and then interpolate to get the integral over the rectangular interval specified for each data point.

Delta s
We use the notation @(@ \Delta s @)@ for the ode_step_size .

a_min, a_max
We use @(@ a_\R{min} @)@ and @(@ a_\R{max} @)@ to denote the minimum and maximum age in the age_table .

t_min, t_max
We use @(@ t_\R{min} @)@ and @(@ t_\R{max} @)@ to denote the minimum and maximum time in the time_table .

Number of Age Points, M
We define @(@ M @)@ as the minimum integers such that @[@ \begin{array}{rcl} a_\R{max} & \leq & a_\R{min} + (M-1) \Delta s \end{array} \; . @]@

Age, a_i
We define @(@ a_i = a_\R{min} + i \Delta s @)@ and the age average grid is @[@ \{ a_i \; | \; i = 0, \ldots , M-1 \} \; . @]@

Number of Time Points, N
We define @(@ N @)@ as the minimum integers such that @[@ \begin{array}{rcl} t_\R{max} & \leq & t_\R{min} + (N-1) \Delta s \end{array} \; . @]@

Time, t_j
We define @(@ t_j = t_\R{min} + j \Delta s @)@ and the ode time grid is @[@ \{ t_j \; | \; j = 0, \ldots , N-1 \} \; . @]@
